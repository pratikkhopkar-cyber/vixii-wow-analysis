The story so far

The first five weeks of the period set a measured baseline: orders held in a 6-9 per week range, AOV averaged £130, and the brand built a steady stream of returning visitors through paid social and organic search. The fundamentals were stable while the team optimised the foundation.

Then W6 changed the picture. Gross sales rose +320% (£804 to £3,375), orders moved from 6 to 20, and returning users surged from 270 to 677 - a +151% jump. Coordinated promotional activity met with notable cultural amplification, producing the strongest week of the period.

What drove W6

The catalyst was external: an influencer's TikTok video featuring VIXII pieces reached the algorithm in the right moment, sending a wave of high-intent traffic to the site. This shows up clearly in the organic and direct numbers - returning users tripled, sessions jumped from 1,076 to 3,214, and brand-led organic queries (vixii, vixii jewellery) climbed sharply in Search Console.

Importantly, the storefront converted the spike well: AOV held at £169, the funnel completed cleanly through cart, and bounce rate did not deteriorate beyond expected levels for cold discovery traffic. The team's foundational work paid off when the moment arrived.

Where the brand is winning

Returns are well managed. April returns sit at 2.4%, comfortably below the 10% target and a clear improvement on March's higher figure. Customers are keeping what they buy - a leading indicator of product fit and expectation alignment.

Organic search has reached a real inflection point. 228 UK clicks at 47% CTR with average position 2.8 reflects strong brand recall. Branded queries dominate today, but unbranded discovery terms (gifts under £250, khaki jewellery, polki bracelet) are now appearing in impressions - a signal that broader category visibility is starting to compound.

Loyalty is concentrated where it should be. Organic Search returns 52% of its visitors and Referral 87%, telling us the brand has true word-of-mouth pull alongside paid reach.

Opportunities to compound

Convert the TikTok moment into durable assets. The W6 traffic surge proves audience appetite. Capturing those visitors into email and remarketing audiences - and producing follow-up creative that extends the moment - would convert a viral spike into a sustained lift. Working with the influencer on a longer programme is worth scoping.

Tune paid efficiency. CPC has trended upward across the period (£0.33 to £0.64) while clicks held flat, and ROAS has averaged below 2 with W6 close to break-even. Refreshed creative and tighter audience targeting are the standard next step; a small test budget on lookalikes derived from W6's high-intent traffic could pay back quickly.

Lean into organic. The unbranded queries showing up in GSC are a roadmap for content. A handful of well-targeted category and gifting pages could capture this rising demand at near-zero variable cost. Pair with structured data and the brand can show up in AI search citations as well.

Closing thought. The trajectory through W6 reflects a brand finding its footing. The team has the foundation in place; the next phase is about building repeatable systems on top of it - so the next viral moment is not a surprise, but an expected lift.
